Examination (Rules & Regulations)

1. The performance of a student in a semester shall be evaluated through continuous class assessment and end semester examination. The continuous assessment shall be based on class tests, assignments/tutorials, quizzes/viva-voce and attendance. The marks for continuous assessment (Sessional marks) shall be awarded at the end of the semester. The end semester examination shall be comprised of written papers, practicals and viva-voce, inspection of certified course work in classes and laboratories, project work, design reports or by means of any combination of these methods.
2. The distribution of marks for sessional, end semester theory papers, practicals and other examinations, seminar, project, industrial training and general proficiency shall be as prescribed.
3. The marks obtained in a subject shall consist of marks allotted in end semester theory paper, practical examination and sessional work.
4. The minimum pass marks in each theory subject (including sessional marks) shall be 40% with a minimum of 30% marks in each theory paper in the end semester examination. If there is no provision of sessional marks in any subject, the minimum pass marks in that subject shall be 30% in the end semester examination.
5. The minimum pass marks in a project/practical subject (including sessional marks if any) shall be 50%.
6. A candidate, in order to pass, must secure 50% marks in the aggregate in a particular academic year inclusive of both semesters of the academic year subjected to conditions as clause 9.2(a).
7. The minimum pass marks in Seminar, Industrial Training and Educational Tour, Viva-Voce etc. shall be 50%.

Workshop for Student’s Career Counselling for Prospective Placements

With a motive to enhance the employability skills of the students, AKTU, Lucknow organized career counselling session in G.L. Bajaj Institute of Technology and Management, Greater Noida. Shri Dinesh Pathak (Senior journalist and renowned career counselor) motivated the students to enhance their confidence level and impart flexibility by acquiring additional knowledge through online courses. He emphasized the need of self-motivation among students and suggested methods to boost their self-confidence. The session was attended by more than 350 students of Mechanical and Civil Engineering students. The energy among the students was evident through their queries during and after the session. This program will have deep impact on the thinking process of students about their career prospects. The institute would like to organize more of such programs in future. Dr. Rajeev Agarwal, Director GLBITM reiterated the need of self-motivation among the students and offered support from institute for such programs in future.
